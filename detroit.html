Detroit Style Pan-Pizza

When you think of good pizza, Detroit doesn't usually come to mind. However, the detroit style pizza has been a mainstay in Detroit for may decades. It was invented at a former speakeasy by Gus and Anna Guerra in Detroit, Michigan. The recipe is what sets this rectangular-shaped pizza apart in the crowd.

Blue-Steel Pan

Detroit Pizza is baked in a blue-steel pan that was typically used in the automotive assembly line to hold nuts and bolts. The steel pan withstands a higher temperature for baking.

Last updated 12/14/24

Focaccia-Like Bread

The pizza dough is double-proofed (baked twice). The crust is a thicker, chewier, which results in a more flavorful and filling pizza crust. The bread is usually made with a sourdough formualtion.

Last updated 12/14/24

Wisconsin Brick Cheese

Wisconsin brick cheese is mild but has a high fat content. The higher fat content allows the cheese to melt easier and flow to the edges of the pan where it caramelizes.

The Detroit style pizza was invented in 1946 by an Italian immigrant named Gus Guerra. He made the pizza dough in a manner that he was accustomed to eating back home in Italy. The crust was thicker and heartier than the pizza crust of other regional pizzas.

In 2012 Detroit style pizza won first place in the international Pizza Expo in Las Vegas. From that moment on, it has gained more notoriety. And it has started to spread nationwide.

Since that fateful first place victory in 2012, the Detroit style pizza places have begun to spring up all over the US. The Detroit style pizza recipes vary slightly, but the pizza is becoming a major player in the pizza world.
