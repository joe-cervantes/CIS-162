Detroit Style Pan-Pizza

When you think of good pizza, Detroit does not usually come to mind. However, the detroit style pizza has been mainstay in Detroit for may decades. It was invented at a former speakeasy by Gus and Anna Guerra in Detroit, Michigan. The recipe is what sets this rectangular-shaped pizzza apart in the crowd.

The Detroit style pizza is made with the pepperoni sitting directly on top of the foccaccia-like bread. The bread is double-proofed and Wisconsin brick cheese, which has a higher fat content. The pizza is baked at 700 Â°F in rectangular pans that were repurposed from the automotive assembly lines.
