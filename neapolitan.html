Neapolitan

Neopolitian pizza is the first ever style of pizza that was invented in the late 16th century in Naples, Italy. In 1889, Raffaele Esposito created the Pizza Margherita for Queen Margherita of Sevoy. He used tomatoes, mozzarella, and basil to reflect the Italian flag.

There are four ingredients that must be used for a pizza to be correctly classified as a Neapolitan style pizza. Marzano tomotoes, finely refined flour that is usually classified as 0 or 00 zero flour, natural mozarella cheese, and fresh basil.

Marzano tomatoes are a vareity of plum tomato that originated in the Campania region of italy. It is known for its rich flavor and its often used in canning.
Flour must be finely ground and is usually classified as 00, which is of the finest flour categories. The flour must be made in Italy.
fresh mozzarella is milky, tender, and mild, with a faint layer of cream beneath the skin that gushes a little when you slice it. It melts evenly, perfectly topping a freshly baked pizza.
The Neapolitan pizza is topped off with a sprinkling of fresh basil. The aroma of fresh basil contrasts the smell of baked tomatoes encased in fresh mozarella cheese. Fresh basil also adds to the presentation with its beautiful green color.


Neapolitan pizza is considered the first style of pizza. This style spawned a subset of Margherita pizza with its inroduction in 1889 and it is still going strong.
